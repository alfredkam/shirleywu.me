    Prologue

    The cross-ethnic socializing function of European chamber music is immense :) at least based on my own experience as a Chinese French Canadian, I found that when someone uses another culture to interpret the same music, they are always able to offer the piece a brand new interpretation, open up a new perspective, which could be a very refreshing experience for both sides :) cultures are attracted to each other because of their differences but accept each other because of their similarities. Germanic music certainly has achieved the absolute perfection in the physical world with its absolute perfect structure but the development doesn't end there, there're still a lot of spaces to grow in the metaphysical, plus it would be considered such an honorable act of kindness if a musician is willing to share this absolute beauty and harmony to everybody they know, including those from other cultures and ethnic backgrounds.

    Barbara Lopez

    D’origine montréalaise, la jeune soprano colorature, Barbara Heath Lopez est diplômée de l’Université McGill, de l’Université d’Ottawa ainsi que du Conservatoire de Musique de Montréal où elle obtenu un premier Prix. Lors de stages en Europe, elle a eu l’occasion d’interpréter de nombreux rôles, notamment ceux de la Reine de la Nuit, Die Zauberflöte (Mozart), le rôle-titre de l’opéra Mireille (Gounod), La Fée dans Cendrillon (Massenet) et Maria dans West Side Story (Bernstein).

    Barbara Heath Lopez a une « présence scénique captivante…[et] excelle dans la colorature, chantant avec une facilité déconcertante, trillant plus vite qu’un oiseau… ». Opera Canada

    La jeune soprano est boursière de l’Austrain-American Vocal Academy, de la Franco-American Vocal Academy, du Centre d’Arts d’Orford et de l’Université d’Ottawa, et s’est classé deuxième au Barry-Alexander International Voice Competition. Elle vient récemment de compléter une série de récitals solos avec la Société d’Art Vocal.

    Originally from Montreal, coloratura soprano Barbara Heath Lopez obtained her Bachelor’s degree from McGill University, her Master’s degree from the University of Ottawa and her Artist Diploma from the Conservatoire de Musique de Montréal.

    Opera Canada described her as having a “commanding stage presence, excell[ing] in the coloratura writing, warbling with apparent ease and trilling faster than any bird…”

    Her credits include having performed as “Mireille” in Gounod’s Mireille, “Lakmé” in Lakmé , “Königin der Nacht” in Die Zauberflöte, “Barbarina” in Le Nozze di Figaro, “Nanetta” in Falstaff ,and “La Fée” in Massenet’s Cendrillon. Additionally, she has recently completed a series of solo recitals with the Société d’Art Vocal de Montréal.


    Christine Menschner

    Born in Germany lyric Soprano Christine Menschner studied voice at the Dresden University of Music „Carl Maria von Weber“ in Germany. After finishing her German State Examination in Music and German Education at the Technical University in Dresden, Christine graduated at the University of Music with a Double Master's degree in Vocal performance and Vocal Education (German Diploma).

    Concerts brought her to Venezuela, Belgium, Italy, the United States and Canada. She worked with conductors such as Tõnu Kaljuste, Denis Menier, Jonathan Velasco, Anton Armstrong, Felipe Izceray, Hans Christoph Rademann, Maria Guinand, Eckehard Stier, Lancelot Fuhry, Mark Rohde and Christian Garbosnik.

    Intensive private Vocal Studies with acclaimed opera singer Lucja Zarzycka (Dresden) and masterclasses with Eduardo Villa (Metroplitan Opera New York), Neil Semer (New York), Roberta Cunningham (Berlin), Dr. Samuel de Palma (Santa Barbara/California, Gwyneth Jones, Franz Grundheber, Karin Mitscherling, Evelyn Herlitzius, Charlotte Lehmann and Dr. Hannelore Seezen-Mundt shaped her vocal abilities and performance skills.

    Christine's first engagement has been in “The Golem Project- a revue about the parabolic velocity of things and human beings” at the reopening of the well known Hellerau European Centre for the arts in Dresden/ Germany.

    Christine performed in several operetta and opera productions such as „The beautiful Helen“ (Offenbach/ P. Hacks), „Sisters“ (T. Fransz), „Die Fledermaus“ (J. Strauss), „ La Bohème“ (G. Puccini), „Mask in Blue“ (F. Raymond), „The Tale of Tsar Saltan“ (R. Korsakov).

    In 2013 Christine gave her debut as Vanella in the Pergolesi opera „Lo frate nn'amorato“ in Rome/ Italy.

    As a soloist Christine performs regularly numerous recitals and Sacred music. Her audience loves the clarity and brilliance of her voice and her natural and honest expression.

    Recently Christine moved to Seattle and serves as an active performer and board member of Ladies Musical Club. She is dedicated to advocate the German speaking music in the community by performing and teaching emerging artists in German Opera, Art Song, Oratorio and diction.


    Maria Sourjko

    A Russian-born pianist, Maria Sourjko studied in Petrozavodsk State Conservatoire. After immigrating to Canada, Ms. Sourjko continued her studies with Jean Desmarais and Jean Saulnier. She also participated in masterclasses given by Richard Raymond, André Laplante, Monique Deschaussées, Jean Paul Sevilla, Jean Philippe Collard, Tatiana Kravchenko and Viktor Portnoi.
