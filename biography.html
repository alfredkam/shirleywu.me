Shirley Wu

Shirley Wu began her piano lessons at Wuhan Academy of Music when she was 4 years old. Every Saturday, for the next 9 years or so, her mother, a former university professor and an amateur violinist, brought Shirley to piano lessons. Shirley’s grandfather, very proficient in the Chinese traditional art Tai Chi and Chinese chess, have always taught the family the importance of arts in a person’s life. When Shirley was 13, her family immigrated to Montreal, Canada. Shirley attended F.A.C.E. (Fine Art Core Education) school, a famous bilingual arts high school in Montreal where she practised as much piano as she could and became deeply attached to this instrument. Shirley decided to make this her career, she graduated from classical piano performance and pure science double D.E.C. from Vanier College in 2009 and classical piano performance from McGill University in 2013.

Shirley’s former piano professors include Su Bin from Wuhan Academy of music, Julliard graduate Noraire Artinian (at Vanier College), Julia G , Luba Z., Ilya P. and Martin K. (at McGill). She was also invited to attend Courchevel summer music festival in the French Alps where she studied with prestigious professor Christopher Elton from Royal Academy of Music UK.

In 2012, Shirley was hired as the pianist / assistant manager at Steinway Montreal for almost one year. She helped out with the Vladimir Horowitz piano while it was on exhibition in Montreal. This gave her the opportunity to broaden her connection in the classical music industry. Her former patrons include Transrex Inc. owner Francois Gelinas’s family, CMC competition sponsor Canimex foundation Mr. Roger Dubois’s family, the Piano Bolduc family etc.

As a performer, Shirley has performed in Tanna Schulich hall, Pollack hall, Salle Claude Champagne, Vanier Auditorium, Rialto theater, Westmount Senior Residence, St Laurent Church etc. Her enthusiasm about piano and classical music keeps influencing ones around her to become supporter of this almost dying art.

Shirley plans to pursue a Master degree of performance science at Royal College of Music in September 2015.

Werner Chan

Werner Chan was born and grew up in Hong Kong. At the age of 5 he started his musical training by taking piano lessons. After high school matriculation, he was accepted by the Hong Kong Academy of Performing Arts to study music. He graduated with the Diploma of Music.

In 2001, Werner Chan received two scholarships, the Conservatory Ovation Award and the International Chancellor’s Award, to study music at the University of Missouri-Kansas City, USA. After his Bachelor’s degree, he furthered his studies and graduated with distinction with a Master’s degree in Music in 2007.

After his musical education, Werner Chan started his career in Montreal where he lived and worked for 3 years. He was teaching in music schools as well as working as accompanist for musicians.

In 2009, under the commission of the Toronto Chinese Dance Company, the original music composed by Werner Chan was used in two dance dramas, The borrowed arrows (草船借箭) and the Liu Yi (柳毅傳書) . The dance drama Liu Yi was performed in the Toronto annual dance festival in 2010.

Since 2011 Werner Chan settled in Toronto and started his own piano studio, Werner Music Academy of music. Apart from giving piano and music theory lessons, Werner has been performing piano recitals in various senior homes and music events organized by music enthusiasts. He was the guest performer of the Opus One Richmond Hill music event held in November 2014. Also he performed as guest performer for piano solo in the OCMS choir concert “Around the World in 80 Minutes” held on 30 th May 2015 in Toronto.

Werner is a registered RCM piano teacher. He is also a member of the Ontario Registered Music Teacher Association (ORMTA).
